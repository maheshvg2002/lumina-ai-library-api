The red dust of Mars whipped against the reinforced glass of Habitat 4. Commander Elias Thorne adjusted his suit's oxygen flow, watching the storm gauge climb into the red zone. They had been on the surface for exactly forty-two days, and this was the worst terra-storm they had seen yet. "Secure the solar arrays," Elias barked into his comms, his voice cutting through the static. If the arrays went down, they would lose heat within the hour. Dr. Aris, the team's botanist, was already rushing towards the airlock. They weren't just fighting for their own survival anymore; the experimental crops in Greenhouse B held the key to establishing a permanent colony. If they failed today, humanity's future in the stars would end before it even began.
